UPI Online Payment Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It usually occurs when cybercriminals trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests. In many cases, the attacker contacts the victim through phone calls, messages, or fake advertisements.

For example, a student may try to sell an item online through a social media marketplace. A scammer pretends to be a buyer and sends a fake payment request through a UPI app. The scammer asks the victim to enter their UPI PIN to "receive money". However, entering the PIN actually approves a payment from the victim’s account.

Students and new online users are common targets because they may not fully understand how digital payments work. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, money is transferred instantly to the scammer's account. Recovering the money can be difficult if the fraud is not reported immediately.

Such incidents can lead to financial loss, emotional stress, and loss of trust in online platforms. It is important for users to understand that UPI PIN should never be shared and should only be used to send money, not to receive it.
