# Project Overview

This repository contains my Digital Literacy Portfolio Project created as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy course. The project was designed to help students understand essential digital skills required in modern academic and professional environments.

As a Student Digital Ambassador, the objective of this project was to explore different digital platforms, practice responsible online communication, and spread awareness about digital safety and cybercrime prevention.

The project includes five tasks, each corresponding to a specific module of the course. These tasks focus on developing practical knowledge related to digital tools, professional online presence, collaboration platforms, communication etiquette, and cybersecurity awareness.

# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

In this task, I created an infographic to explain the concept of digital literacy and its importance for students.

The infographic was designed using Canva, which provides easy-to-use templates and visual elements. The infographic highlights the following key topics:

Meaning of digital literacy
Useful digital tools for students
Safe internet practices
Importance of maintaining a professional online presence

The purpose of this visual resource is to help students quickly understand the importance of responsible and effective use of digital technologies.

The infographic image is available in the task-1-presentation folder.

# Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio

In this task, I created my initial student digital portfolio by setting up accounts on important professional and technical platforms.

The platforms I created profiles on include:
github – Used for storing and managing code repositories and projects.
LinkedIn – A professional networking platform where students can connect with recruiters, mentors, and professionals.
Kaggle – A platform used for data science learning, datasets, and machine learning competitions.
Screenshots of the created profiles are included in the task-2-portfolio folder.

# Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

This task involved exploring online platforms that support coding practice and collaboration.

Coding Practice Platform

I created an account on HackerRank, a platform used for improving programming and problem-solving skills. I completed a beginner-level coding challenge to gain experience with how coding platforms work.

The screenshot of the completed challenge is available in the task-3-platforms folder.

# Task 4 – Email Etiquette and Professional Communication

In this task, I practiced professional email writing and created guidelines for responsible social media use.

Two professional emails were drafted:

A student requesting an assignment deadline extension from a professor.
A student expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity.

These emails demonstrate proper email etiquette, including:

Clear subject line
Professional greeting
Well-structured message body
Appropriate closing and signature

Additionally, a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist was created to guide students on responsible online behavior.

All files related to this task are included in the task-4-email-etiquette folder.

# Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study

This task focused on understanding cybercrime and promoting digital safety.

I selected UPI Online Payment Fraud, which is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting digital payment users in India.

The case study explains:

What the cybercrime is
How the fraud typically occurs
Who is commonly targeted
The consequences of such attacks

A Stay Safe Online checklist was also created to help students prevent cybercrime incidents.

Important reporting resources included in the checklist:

National Cyber Crime Portal – https://cybercrime.gov.in
Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930

All files related to this task are available in the task-5-cybercrime folder.
